## 2. APPRAISAL PROCESS KNOWLEDGE

### The Appraisal Process (John A. Voelpel III)
**Book:** "The Appraisal Process: Resolution of Disputed Insurance Claims"
**Author background:** One of Florida's busiest property insurance umpires. Taught Windstorm Insurance Network appraisal courses for 20+ years (sold out).

#### Florida Appraisal Framework (F.S. 627.7015)

| Stage | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Trigger** | Either policyholder OR insurer can invoke the appraisal clause |
| **Appraiser selection** | Each party selects an independent appraiser. Policyholder must name theirs within 20 days of written demand. |
| **Appraiser role** | Evaluate damage, review estimates, determine fair value independently |
| **Umpire selection** | If appraisers can't agree on value, they jointly select a neutral umpire |
| **Court appointment** | If appraisers can't agree on an umpire, court appoints one |
| **Decision rule** | Agreement by any 2 of 3 (appraiser + appraiser, or appraiser + umpire) = final and binding |
| **Overturning** | Award can only be overturned for fraud or collusion between panel members |
| **Cost split** | Policyholder pays own appraiser + 50% of umpire. Carrier pays own appraiser + 50% of umpire. |
| **Umpire rates** | $250-$450/hour typical |
| **Pre-appraisal requirement** | F.S. 627.7015 requires carriers to offer mediation BEFORE demanding appraisal. Failure = waiver of appraisal right. |

#### Umpire Selection Best Practices

1. **Vet before agreeing.** Research umpire's complete background, work history, and potential bias.
2. **Watch for carrier loyalty.** Umpires with extensive insurance company work history may be compromised.
3. **Get it in writing.** Signed agreement by both appraisers AND the umpire before proceeding.
4. **Agree early.** If appraisers disagree on value, they'll likely disagree on umpire choice too -- negotiate umpire selection early.
5. **Know the small pool.** The "cottage niche" of qualified umpires is small -- know who's available in your market.
6. **The umpire decides.** Many cases come down to the umpire's call. This selection is arguably the most important strategic decision in the entire appraisal.

## 3. XACTIMATE ESTIMATING BEST PRACTICES

### Critical Accuracy Factors

| Factor | Impact | Eve Action |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **Room dimensions** | 1ft error per wall = 16% area error, cascading to sheetrock, paint, baseboard, flooring | Flag dimension discrepancies in carrier estimates |
| **Waste factors** | Carpet: min 5% over room area. Complex patterns/borders = higher. Roofing waste factors vary by complexity. | Check waste factor presence and adequacy |
| **OH&P (Overhead & Profit)** | ~20% for single family homes. Can be in line items OR bottom of estimate -- know where it's included. | Verify OH&P inclusion and placement |
| **Regional pricing** | Always specify job region -- pricing varies significantly by geography | Ensure FL-specific pricing is applied |
| **Xactimate pricing bias** | Pricing is often too low, based on median surveys that don't reflect current market conditions | Flag when Xactimate prices lag actual market rates |

### Line Item Best Practices

1. **Search, don't scroll.** Use specific keywords to find line items efficiently.
2. **Custom line items** when predefined ones don't match -- accuracy over convenience.
3. **Document justifications** for every line item selection. This is your best defense in disputes.
4. **Include project-specific details** and client preferences in justification notes.
5. **Transparency wins.** Clear documentation streamlines approval and reduces disputes.

### Common Carrier Estimate Errors to Flag

- Underestimated room dimensions
- Missing or inadequate waste factors
- OH&P excluded or buried
- Generic pricing not matching local FL market
- Missing line items for work that will clearly be required
- Improper categorization of damage types

## 4. NEGOTIATION TACTICS & CARRIER STRATEGIES

### What Carriers Do (Know the Enemy)

| Carrier Tactic | Description | PA Counter-Strategy |
|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Delay disguised as procedure** | "Waiting on paperwork," "investigating," "other department" | Document every delay. Reference FL 90-day requirement. |
| **Document bombardment** | Redundant/irrelevant document requests to exhaust policyholder | Respond promptly but challenge irrelevant requests in writing. |
| **Low initial offers** | Opening positions, not final determinations | Never accept first offer. Counter with documented evidence. |
| **Adjuster training in minimization** | Carrier adjusters are trained to reduce settlement values | Match their training with superior documentation and policy knowledge. |
| **Psychological pressure** | Encourage acceptance without professional representation | Immediately engage PA. Carriers know PA involvement = higher payouts. |
| **Engineer report manipulation** | Honest field reports altered to support predetermined outcomes (Quinn/APA) | Request original field notes. Compare field report vs. final report. |

### PA Negotiation Principles

1. **Documentation is ammunition.** Photos, videos (before/during/after), receipts, timestamped correspondence. Every undocumented item is money left on the table.
2. **FL 90-day rule.** Insurers must acknowledge, investigate, and resolve within 90 days of proof of loss. Use this as leverage.
3. **Never accept first offer.** Initial offers are opening positions, not fair value determinations.
4. **The claims process IS a negotiation.** "Give up a little to get a little" (Perri). Strategic concession, not capitulation.
5. **PA involvement = 747% average increase.** Citizens Property Insurance data: $2,029 average without PA vs. $17,187 with PA.
6. **Attorney collaboration.** PAs document/negotiate; attorneys enforce the law. Both are needed in FL's challenging insurance climate.
7. **Track carrier deadlines.** Missed insurer deadlines and incomplete explanations become key exhibits.

## 5. SUPPLEMENT STRATEGY

### When to Supplement

- Initial estimate missed damage (discovered during tear-out/remediation)
- Additional damage found after mitigation
- Market prices have increased since original estimate
- Carrier adjuster used incorrect line items or pricing
- New evidence (expert reports, additional inspections) supports higher value

### Supplement Best Practices

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Pre-supplement documentation** | Photos before mitigation, during tear-out, pre-paint. Labeled angles + timestamps. |
| **Request reinspection** | Invite carrier's adjuster to see added scope in place -- don't just send paper. |
| **Tie proof to line items** | Each supplement line item needs clear, documented justification. |
| **Carrier supplement forms** | Many carriers require specific supplement request forms -- use them to open review. |
| **Strong files win** | "Strong files win supplements." Quality of documentation directly correlates with approval rate. |
| **Track denials** | If supplement denied, the denial letter + claim file notes become key exhibits. Keep in timeline log. |

### FL Supplement Compensation Rules

- PA fee on supplements: up to **20%** (vs. 10% on initial claims)
- FL law allows supplemental claims when new evidence is presented
- Professional estimates, reports, and expert advocacy challenge carrier decisions
