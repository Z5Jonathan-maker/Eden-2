# SECTION 1: CLAIMS HANDLING BEST PRACTICES (Source: Barry Zalma)

## 1.1 Core Claims Handling Principles

### Thorough Investigation Standards
- Insurers must assign qualified field adjusters early to meet insureds at loss locations and create complete damage scopes
- Every policy provision must be read and understood to determine applicable coverage
- Staff dedication to excellence reduces litigation and breach of contract allegations
- Good faith obligation continues throughout the entire claims resolution process

> "The customs and practice of the first party property insurance claims industry is to assign a field adjuster...who can meet at the loss location with the insured." -- Barry Zalma

### Fair Claims Settlement Practices
- California's Fair Claims Settlement Practices Regulations (established 1993) serve as the industry gold standard
- Every insurance claims person must certify knowledge of regulations annually by September 1
- Non-compliance can result in fines up to $55,000 per violation
- Potential loss of certificate of authority to conduct business
- These standards apply broadly as minimum benchmarks even outside California

### Insurance as Contract of Utmost Good Faith
- Both parties must act with good faith throughout the relationship
- **Insurer's obligation**: Pay promised indemnity when a covered loss occurs, excluding specified perils
- **Insured's obligation**: Honestly disclose risks, truthfully present claims, and cooperate in investigations
- Insurance fraud costs the industry between $87 billion and $300 billion annually

## 1.2 Bad Faith Insurance Practices

### Definition and Scope
Bad faith occurs when an insurer fails to honor its duty of good faith and fair dealing. Originally designed to protect insureds from insurer abuse, the tort of bad faith has become a significant area of insurance litigation.

### Key Bad Faith Indicators
1. Unreasonable delay in claim investigation or payment
2. Failure to properly investigate claims
3. Denying claims without reasonable basis
4. Lowball settlement offers not supported by facts
5. Failure to communicate claim status to insured
6. Misrepresenting policy provisions
7. Refusing to pay claims when liability is clear

### Zalma's Position on Bad Faith
- Breach of contract should remain a contractual matter, not a tort action
- Bad faith tort has been exploited by fraudulent claimants
- Wrongful rescission attempts may constitute breach of implied covenant of good faith
- Proper investigation and legal counsel are critical before denying any claim

## 1.3 Insurance Fraud Detection

### Red Flags for Fraud
- Claims submitted shortly after policy inception or coverage increase
- Insured cannot provide documentation of claimed losses
- Insured has history of multiple claims
- Claim amount suspiciously close to policy limits
- Insured provides conflicting statements
- Loss occurs during financial difficulties
- Insured is overly cooperative or overly hostile

### Fraud Investigation Best Practices
- Most frauds succeed without the insurer suspecting deception
- When insurers invest effort in fraud reduction, claims drop logarithmically
- SIU (Special Investigation Unit) personnel must be properly trained
- Documentation of all investigation steps is critical
- Examinations Under Oath (EUO) are powerful fraud detection tools

### Rescission as Fraud Remedy
- Rescission is an equitable remedy rooted in British common law (1766, *Carter v. Boehm*)
- Allows insurers to void policies based on material misrepresentation or concealment
- Available regardless of whether deception was intentional fraud or innocent misrepresentation (in most states)
- Must be exercised with care -- wrongful rescission exposes insurer to tort damages including punitive damages
- Claims professionals must understand rescission fundamentals

### Bases for Rescission
1. Misrepresentation of material fact(s)
2. Concealment of material fact(s)
3. Mistake of material fact(s)
4. Mistake of law

## 1.4 Key Case Law Lessons (Recent, from Zalma Blog)

### Public Adjuster Anti-Assignment Clauses
**Barbato v. Interstate Fire & Casualty** (2026) -- Court upheld insurer's right to include Anti-Public-Adjuster (APA) clauses in policies. APA clauses are not expressly prohibited by insurance law. Insurers can contractually prevent public adjusters from representing insureds.

### Material Misrepresentation on Applications
**Primerica v. Bucio** (2026) -- $614,000 life insurance policy rescinded due to concealed drug abuse history. Insurance requires utmost good faith; false answers about material facts justify complete policy rescission.

### Anti-Assignment Clauses
**Ciotola v. RSA Insurance Group** (2026) -- Anti-assignment provisions prevent end-runs around arbitration requirements. $9 million consent judgment assignment failed due to policy language.

### Statute of Limitations
**Ex parte Alfa Mutual v. Kinsman** (2026) -- Hurricane Katrina claim filed 17 years after loss was time-barred. Alabama Supreme Court granted rare Writ of Mandamus.

### Pro Se Litigation Risks
**Hsu v. State Farm** (2026) -- Self-represented homeowners lost bad faith and fraud claims; only breach of contract survived. Non-lawyers pursuing insurance litigation fare poorly.

### Fraudulent Billing Schemes
**Connecticut General v. East Coast Advanced Plastic Surgery** (2026) -- No statutory right to commit fraud against an insurer. No Surprises Act provides no private right of action for fraudulent billers.

# SECTION 2: POLICYHOLDER ADVOCACY STRATEGIES (Source: United Policyholders)

## 2.1 Top Insurance Claim Tips

### Foundational Principles
- Document everything from the moment of loss
- Communicate with your insurer in writing
- Keep a detailed claim diary
- Understand your policy before disaster strikes
- Don't accept first offers without analysis
- Seek professional help when needed

## 2.2 Hurricane Property Damage Claims

### Coverage Clarifications
- **Not all hurricane damage is excluded** -- insurers often incorrectly deny claims by blaming flood exclusions
- What matters: location, damage causation, policy wording, state laws, and persistence
- If water entered through damaged openings (roof, siding, windows), wind triggered the damage and it should be covered
- Wind-driven rain penetrates approximately one inch per 10 mph of wind speed
- Homes damaged by storm surge still experienced wind damage first -- wind bears causation responsibility

### Adjuster Relationship Management
- Adjusters represent profit-driven companies, not policyholders
- Their goal is closing claims quickly with minimal payouts
- Never accept denials without seeking second opinions on damage causation and policy interpretation
- Request copies of all adjuster reports, photographs, and evaluations

### Common Post-Hurricane Problems
1. Frustrating delays in claim processing
2. Rotating adjuster assignments (loss of institutional knowledge)
3. Incorrect coverage information
4. Lowball repair estimates and settlement offers
5. Poor treatment despite long-standing policies

### Coverage for Specific Damage Types
- **Food spoilage** from electrical outages -- covered under standard homeowners
- **Power surge damage** related to storms -- covered
- **Motor vehicle damage** -- covered under comprehensive auto (separate policy)
- **Falling tree damage** -- covered under homeowners
- **Wind-driven rain** entering through compromised building envelope -- covered

### Items Frequently Underpaid
- **Matching**: Insurer owes restoration to pre-loss condition; repairs must appear uniform and consistent
- **Overhead & Profit (O&P)**: Contractors charge 10% overhead plus 10% profit; withholding O&P until completion is unfair and illegal in some states
- **Depreciation**: Subjective, not scientific. Non-depreciable items include studs, cement, rebar, framing (200+ year lifespan)
- **Building code upgrades**: Coverage exists for mandatory code compliance during reconstruction
- **Foundation inspections**: Concrete failure from storm damage may not be visible to untrained eyes

### Additional Living Expense (ALE) Strategies
- Negotiate to "cash out" ALE coverage limits when rental housing is unavailable
- Use funds to purchase trailers or motor homes instead of paying rent
- Request extensions of ALE benefits when reconstruction delays occur
- Additional living expenses require separate tracking and documentation

## 2.3 Dispute Resolution Strategies

### Informal Resolution
1. Read complete policy language and exclusion wording
2. Gather expert assessments (forensic engineers, damage specialists)
3. Organize documentation systematically
4. Write to company executives, not just adjusters
5. Contact state Insurance Commissioner's office
6. Document all communications in a claim diary

### Mediation
- Framework for resolving claim disputes outside litigation
- Less expensive and faster than court proceedings
- Both parties must agree to participate
- Results may or may not be binding depending on agreement

### Insurance Appraisal
- Available for resolving valuation disputes (amount owed, not coverage)
- Each side selects an appraiser; appraisers select an umpire
- Decision is binding on both parties
- Policyholders can and do win in appraisal proceedings

### Examinations Under Oath (EUO)
- Insurer-requested sworn examinations
- Preparation is critical -- similar to deposition
- Failure to comply may void coverage
- Public adjusters should prepare clients for EUO process

### Litigation Considerations
- Last resort after informal methods fail
- Seek experienced insurance attorney (contingency fee common)
- Realistic expectations: litigation takes months to years
- Insurance bad faith claims allow tort damages in most states

## 2.4 Hiring Professional Help

### Public Adjuster Evaluation Criteria
- Verify valid public adjuster license for your state
- Confirm whether they work independently or under another firm's license
- Check years of experience and length of service in state
- Verify physical location and service capabilities
- Assess communication style and professionalism

### Public Adjuster Fee Guidance
- Most work on contingency (5-15% of recovered funds)
- Fees are capped in some states (Florida: 10% emergency, 20% non-emergency)
- Fee amounts should reflect loss size, type, and claim status
- Clarify whether previously collected funds affect fee calculation
- Clarify if fees apply to ALE portions

### Red Flags When Hiring
- High-pressure sales tactics
- Rushing into hiring decisions
- Unclear fee arrangements
- Vague service plans for out-of-area claims
- Unwillingness to provide references
- Lack of transparent communication

### Reference Check Questions
1. Did the adjuster achieve better results than going solo?
2. Did they maintain regular contact throughout the process?
3. Did they add genuine value justifying their fee?
4. Did they address ALE and fair rental value claims?

## 3.1 FAPIA Overview

The **Florida Association of Public Insurance Adjusters (FAPIA)** is the primary professional organization for licensed public adjusters in Florida. FAPIA promotes:
- Ethical standards in public adjusting
- Continuing education for members
- Legislative advocacy for fair PA regulations
- Consumer protection through professional standards

## 3.2 Public Adjuster Code of Ethics

### Core Ethical Principles
1. **Client First**: Always act in the insured's best interest
2. **Honesty**: Never misrepresent facts, coverage, or potential outcomes
3. **Competence**: Maintain sufficient knowledge and skills to handle claims properly
4. **Transparency**: Full disclosure of fees, processes, and relationships
5. **Independence**: Avoid conflicts of interest with contractors, vendors, or insurers
6. **Compliance**: Follow all state licensing laws and regulations

### Prohibited Conduct (per FL Statute 626.8698)
- Receiving payment from unfair or deceptive practices
- Kickbacks or split-fee arrangements with non-adjusters
- Payment for services not performed
- Soliciting or taking advantage of vulnerable, emotional, or upset persons
- Violating any ethical rule of the department

## 3.3 Professional Standards for FL Public Adjusters

### Licensing Requirements (per FL Statute 626.865)
- Must be natural person at least 18 years of age
- U.S. citizen or legal alien with work authorization
- Demonstrated trustworthy business reputation
- Sufficient experience, training, or instruction in adjusting
- Must pass public adjuster examination
- Must file $50,000 surety bond
- 6 months prior experience in specified adjuster roles

### Continuing Education
- Florida requires continuing education credits for license renewal
- FAPIA offers CE courses and annual conference sessions
- Topics include: claims estimation, policy interpretation, ethics, and FL statute updates

### Business Operations Standards
- Each business location must designate a primary adjuster (FL 626.8695)
- Primary adjuster is responsible for supervision of all individuals acting as adjusters
- Adjusting firm cannot operate without designated primary adjuster
- 90-day grace period to designate new primary adjuster if current one leaves

### Apprentice Requirements (per FL 626.8651)
- Must be licensed as all-lines adjuster
- Must file $50,000 surety bond
- Firm may not maintain more than 4 apprentices simultaneously
- Supervising PA responsible for only 1 apprentice at a time
- Apprentice has same authority as supervising PA except cannot execute contracts

## 3.4 Best Practices for Florida PAs

### Claim Handling Protocol
1. **Initial Contact**: Respond within 24 hours of client inquiry
2. **Inspection**: Thorough on-site damage assessment with photo/video documentation
3. **Policy Review**: Complete analysis of all applicable coverages and exclusions
4. **Estimate Preparation**: Detailed Xactimate estimate within 60 days (statutory requirement)
5. **Claim Filing**: Prompt notice to insurer with all supporting documentation
6. **Negotiation**: Evidence-based negotiation with insurer adjusters
7. **Resolution**: Pursue appraisal or mediation if negotiation fails

### Documentation Standards
- All estimates must be itemized with per-unit repair costs (equipment, materials, labor, supplies)
- Estimates must be retained for 5 years
- Contracts must be provided to insurers within 7 days of execution
- Written estimates must be available to claimants, insurers, and department on request

### Client Communication
- Provide unaltered contract copy at execution
- Explain cancellation rights clearly
- Provide separate disclosure document before contract signing
- Keep client informed of claim progress
- Never restrict insurer access to insured or property

# SECTION 4: FLORIDA INSURANCE STATUTES (Complete Text of Key Sections)

## 4.1 FL Statute 626.854 -- Public Adjuster: Definition, Duties, and Prohibitions

### (1) Definition
A "public adjuster" encompasses any individual (except licensed attorneys exempt under s. 626.860) who, for compensation or value:
- Prepares, completes, or files insurance claims for insureds or third-party claimants
- Acts on behalf of insureds in claim negotiations or settlements
- Advertises adjuster services
- Solicits, investigates, or adjusts claims on behalf of adjusters or claimants

**Exclusions**: Persons photographing damaged property or performing duties under other professional licenses without claim adjustment involvement.

### (2) Exemptions
Does NOT apply to:
- (a) Licensed healthcare providers filing health insurance claims for patients
- (b) Licensed health insurance agents assisting with coverage questions, coding, balance billing
- (c) Persons filing health claims without compensation

### (3) Legal Advice Prohibition
Public adjusters CANNOT:
- Provide legal advice
- Represent parties in bodily injury, death, or noneconomic damage claim negotiations

### (4) Definition of "Insured"
For statutory purposes, "insured" means only the policyholder and named beneficiaries in the policy.

### (5) Solicitation Time Restrictions
Public adjusters may only solicit insureds or claimants **Monday through Saturday, between 8 a.m. and 8 p.m.**

### (6) Contract Requirements (Post-July 1, 2023)
- (a) Fee collection requires written contracts with the insured or their legal representative
- (b) Third-party service contracts require prior written insured consent if costs burden the insured
- (c) Unauthorized third-party service arrangements must be paid by the adjuster, not charged to insured
- (d) When representing third parties, fees must be paid by those parties, not the insured

### (7) Cancellation Rights
- **Standard**: Insured may cancel within **10 days** of execution without penalty
- **Emergency declarations**: Cancellation period extends to **30 days after loss** or 10 days after execution, whichever is longer
- Contracts cancellable without penalty if written estimates not provided within 60 days (unless beyond adjuster's control)
- Cancellation notices must be submitted in writing via certified mail (return receipt requested)

**Required contract language (18-point bold):**
> "You, the insured, may cancel this contract for any reason without penalty or obligation to you within 10 days after the date of this contract. If this contract was entered into based on events that are the subject of a declaration of a state of emergency by the Governor, you may cancel this contract for any reason without penalty or obligation to you within 30 days after the date of loss or 10 days after the date on which the contract is executed, whichever is longer. You may also cancel the contract without penalty or obligation to you if I, as your public adjuster, fail to provide you and your insurer a copy of a written estimate within 60 days of the execution of the contract, unless the failure to provide the estimate within 60 days is caused by factors beyond my control, in accordance with s. 627.70131(5)(a)2., Florida Statutes. The 60-day cancellation period for failure to provide a written estimate shall cease on the date I have provided you with the written estimate."

### (8) Deceptive Advertising Prohibition
Public adjusters CANNOT circulate untrue, deceptive, or misleading advertisements.

Deceptive statements include:
1. Invitations to submit claims for uncovered damage
2. Claims offering monetary inducements for submissions
3. Statements claiming "no risk" in claim submissions
4. Use of logos or language implying governmental agency involvement or endorsement

**Required disclaimer (bold capitals, minimum body-text typeface):**
> "THIS IS A SOLICITATION FOR BUSINESS. IF YOU HAVE HAD A CLAIM FOR AN INSURED PROPERTY LOSS OR DAMAGE AND YOU ARE SATISFIED WITH THE PAYMENT BY YOUR INSURER, YOU MAY DISREGARD THIS ADVERTISEMENT."

### (9) Loan Prohibition
Public adjusters, apprentices, and representatives CANNOT provide monetary loans or advances to clients.

### (10) Merchandise Gift Limitation
Cannot offer merchandise gifts exceeding $25 for advertising or to induce contract execution.

### (11) Compensation Limitations

| Claim Type | Maximum Fee |
|------------|-------------|
| Emergency-declaration claims (first year) | **10%** of claim payments |
| Non-emergency claims | **20%** of claim payments |
| Claims paid at policy limits within 14 days of loss or 10 days after contract | **1%** |
| Claims paid before contract execution | **0%** |
| Reopened/supplemental claims | **20%** of NEW additional payments only |

Additional rules:
- Policy deductibles CANNOT form compensation bases
- ALE compensation requires separate written agreement with specific disclosure language
- Compensation rates CANNOT increase solely because claims become litigated
- Circumventing compensation limits violates s. 626.8698

### (12) Written Estimate Requirements
- Must provide written loss estimates within **60 days** of contract execution
- Must include itemized per-unit repair costs (equipment, materials, labor, supplies) per industry standards
- Estimates must be retained **5 years**
- Made available to claimants, insurers, and department upon request

### (13) Referral Compensation Prohibition
Cannot accept business referrals involving compensation agreements with referral sources. Only exception: referring other licensed public adjusters.

### (14) Insurer Meeting and Inspection Notice
Company adjusters, independent adjusters, attorneys, investigators must provide **48-hour advance notice** before accessing insureds or inspecting property. Insureds may deny access without proper notice (but may waive requirement).

### (15) Claim Handling Obligations
- Must ensure prompt claim notice to insurers
- Must provide contracts to insurers
- Must keep property available for loss inspection
- Must allow insurer interviews with insureds
- (a) Insurers CANNOT exclude public adjusters from in-person meetings
- (b) Public adjusters CANNOT restrict insurer access to insureds or properties
- (c) Public adjusters CANNOT obstruct or prevent timely insurer inspections

### (16) Contractor Licensing
Licensed contractors CANNOT advertise, solicit, offer, or perform public adjuster services without PA licensure. May recommend insureds contact their insurers regarding coverage or discuss repair bids for usual-and-customary fees.

### (17) Salvage Property Interest Prohibition
Cannot acquire salvaged property interests without written insured consent via signed affidavit.

### (18) Repair Authority Restriction
Cannot acquire contractual authority to select repair contractors or service providers when costs exceed adjusting service compensation.

### (19) Residential Property Limitation
Subsections (5)-(18) apply only to **residential property insurance** and condominium unit owner policies per s. 718.111(11).

### (20) Unlicensed Practice Prohibition
Except for licensed attorneys and public adjusters, NO person may, for compensation:
- Prepare, complete, or file insurance claims
- Negotiate or settle claims
- Advertise public adjuster services
- Solicit, investigate, or adjust claims

### (21) Administrative Enforcement
Department may pursue administrative actions and impose fines against unlicensed claim adjusters.

### (22) Non-Contract Solicitation
Public adjusters soliciting claims but not executing contracts CANNOT charge for any related services.

### (23) Roof Damage Inducement Prohibition
- Cannot offer rebates, gifts, gift cards, cash, coupons, deductible waivers, or other inducements for roof inspections or claims
- Violations: fines up to **$10,000 per act** (or **$20,000** during gubernatorial emergencies)
- Non-licensees engaging in prohibited conduct face unlicensed practice penalties

## 4.2 FL Statute 626.865 -- Public Adjuster Qualifications and Bond

### (1) Licensing Requirements
The department must issue a public adjuster license to applicants who meet:

- **(a) Age and Citizenship**: Natural person at least 18 years old; U.S. citizen or legal alien with work authorization
- **(b) Trustworthiness**: Demonstrated business reputation ensuring fair conduct in good faith without public detriment
- **(c) Professional Knowledge**: Sufficient experience, training, or instruction concerning adjusting of damages or losses under insurance contracts; understanding of relevant state insurance law
- **(d) Prior Experience**: Must have served continuously for 6 months in specified roles including nonresident public adjuster, apprentice, independent adjuster, or company employee adjuster

### (2) Bond Requirements
- Must file **$50,000 surety bond** guaranteeing faithful performance
- Bond must authorize department recovery for fraud or unfair practices
- Bond must remain valid for **1 year after license expiration**
- 30-day termination notice provisions required

### (3) Examination
All applicants must pass the public adjuster examination, including those seeking reinstatement or new licenses following prior suspension, termination, or expiration.

## 4.3 FL Statute 626.8651 -- Public Adjuster Apprentice

### (1)(a) Appointment Requirements
Department shall issue appointment as PA apprentice to a licensee who:
1. Is licensed as an all-lines adjuster under s. 626.866
2. Has filed $50,000 surety bond with the department
3. Maintains bond unimpaired throughout appointment

### (1)(b) Bond Terms
Bond must be in favor of the department; specifically authorizes recovery for fraud or unfair practices. Aggregate liability cannot exceed bond amount. 30-day written notice required for termination.

### (2) Supervision Limits
- Appointing PA firm may not maintain more than **4 apprentices** simultaneously
- Supervising PA may only be responsible for **1 apprentice** at a time
- Supervising PA is accountable for apprentice's acts related to PA business
- Exception: firms adjusting claims primarily for multi-state commercial entities

### (3) Apprentice Authority
- Has same authority as employing PA/firm EXCEPT cannot execute contracts
- Must be licensed as all-lines adjuster
- Must hold current appointment by licensed PA firm with designated primary adjuster

## 4.4 FL Statute 626.8695 -- Primary Adjuster

### (1) Designation Requirement
Each business location must designate a primary adjuster licensed and appointed to adjust insurance claims.

### (2) Filing Requirements
Adjusting firm and each branch must designate primary adjuster and file name, license number, and physical address with department. Changes effective upon notification (within 30 days).

### (3) Definition
"Primary adjuster" = licensed adjuster responsible for supervision of all individuals within an adjusting firm location who act as adjusters. May be designated for more than one location provided no unlicensed person engages in adjuster activity when adjuster is not physically present.

### (4) "Adjusting Firm" Definition
A location where an independent or public adjuster is engaged in the business of insurance.

### (5) Employer Compliance
Department may suspend/revoke primary adjuster's license if firm employs or contracts any person whose license has been denied, suspended, or revoked.

### (6) Accountability
Primary adjuster is accountable for misconduct by persons under direct supervision. Not criminally liable unless personally committed the act or knew/should have known of the violation.

### (7) Employment Restrictions
Department may suspend/revoke license of adjuster employed by person whose license is currently suspended or revoked.

### (8) Continuous Operation
Firm may not conduct business without designated primary adjuster. If primary adjuster leaves and replacement not designated within **90 days**, firm license automatically expires on day 91.

### (9) License Verification
Firm may check licensure status by submitting appointment request within 5 working days of hire. If department notifies of suspension/revocation, primary adjuster's license protected if unlicensed person immediately dismissed.

## 4.5 FL Statute 626.8795 -- Conflict of Interest Prohibition

A public adjuster may NOT:
- Participate directly or indirectly in reconstruction, repair, or restoration of damaged property subject to a claim they adjust
- Engage in activities reasonably construed as conflict of interest
- Solicit or accept remuneration of any kind from related parties
- Have financial interest in salvage, repair, or any business entity obtaining business connected to claims they adjust

## 4.6 FL Statute 626.8796 -- PA Contracts, Disclosure, and Fraud Statement

### (1) Contract Format Requirements
- Must be in writing, minimum **12-point type**
- Titled **"Public Adjuster Contract"**
- Must display fraud statement in minimum **18-point bold type** before signature space
- References s. 817.234 (felony penalties for fraudulent claims)

### (2) Required Contract Contents
- PA's full name, permanent business address, phone, email, license number
- PA firm's full name and license number
- Insured's full name, street address, phone, email with brief loss description
- Compensation percentage in minimum **18-point bold type**
- Claim type designation
- Insured initials on each unsigned page
- Signatures of PA and all named insureds
- Signature date
- Unaltered copy to insured at execution; to insurer within **7 days**

### (3) Compensation Timing
PA cannot receive compensation before insured receives unaltered contract copy or contract submitted to insurer. Receipt proof maintained for **5 years**.

### (4) Rescission for Late Estimates
Insured may rescind if PA has not submitted written estimate to insurer within **60 days** (unless factors beyond PA's control).

### (5) Cancellation Period
Terminates when estimate is provided.

### (6) Required Pre-Contract Disclosure Document
Must address:
- Definitions of adjuster types
- PA is NOT insurer representative
- Insured's right to hire or decline PA
- Insured's right to direct communication with all parties
- PA compensation is insured's responsibility
- Unaltered contract copy required at execution
- Cancellation rights (30 days or 10 days, whichever longer for emergency events)

### (7) Non-Compliance
Non-compliant contracts are **invalid and unenforceable**.

## 4.7 FL Statute 626.8698 -- Disciplinary Guidelines for PAs

Department may deny, suspend, or revoke license and impose fines up to **$5,000 per act** for:

1. Violating any provision of the chapter or department rule/order
2. Receiving payment from unfair or deceptive practices
3. Receiving kickbacks, split-fee arrangements with non-PAs, or payment for unperformed services
4. Violating s. 316.066 or s. 817.234
5. Soliciting or taking advantage of vulnerable, emotional, or upset persons
6. Violating any ethical rule of the department

## 4.8 FL Statute 626.866 -- All-Lines Adjuster Qualifications

### License Requirements
- **(1)** Natural person, 18+ years, U.S. citizen or legal alien with work authorization and bona fide state residency
- **(2)** Trustworthy character and business reputation
- **(3)** Adequate experience/training in adjusting damage/loss claims (excluding life and annuity); understanding of contract terms, policy provisions, and FL insurance laws
- **(4)** Must pass written examinations or qualify for exemptions under s. 626.221
- **(5)** Applicable license fee must be paid

---

## 4.9 FL Statute 626.860 -- Attorneys at Law Exemption

Attorneys duly licensed to practice law in Florida, in good standing with The Florida Bar, are NOT required to be licensed as adjusters to adjust or participate in adjustment of any claim, loss, or damage arising under insurance policies or contracts.

```
│   └── No response → Escalate to supervisor/department
│
├── Dispute Resolution:
│   ├── Mediation → Less costly, faster resolution
│   ├── Appraisal → For valuation disputes (binding)
│   └── Litigation → Refer to attorney (fee cannot increase)
│
└── Throughout Process:
    ├── Maintain detailed claim diary
    ├── All communications in writing
    ├── Keep property available for inspection
    └── Allow insurer access to insured
```
