## 1. FLORIDA REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

### 1.1 Licensing Requirements (FL DFS)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| License Type | 3-20 Public Adjuster |
| Regulator | Florida Department of Financial Services (DFS) |
| Surety Bond | $50,000 minimum |
| Background | Fingerprinting + background check required |
| Exam | State licensing exam mandatory |
| Apprenticeship | Required training period before full licensure |
| CE Renewal | Every 2 years by last day of birth month |
| CE Hours (< 6 yrs) | 24 hours per 2-year cycle |
| CE Hours (6+ yrs) | Reduced hours (exact per DFS schedule) |
| Law & Ethics CE | 4-hour course (designation 5-320) mandatory each cycle |
| Elective Restriction | Only courses approved for PA or General Lines (P&C) count (effective July 31, 2023) |

### 1.2 Fee Limits

| Scenario | Maximum Fee |
|----------|-------------|
| Standard claims | 20% of claim payment |
| Emergency-declared claims (1st year) | 10% of claim payment |
| Emergency-declared claims (after 1 year) | 20% of claim payment |
| Claims at/exceeding policy limits | 1% (per HB 1185) |
| Pre-contract payments | 0% -- cannot charge for payments made before contract execution |

### 1.3 Contract Requirements (Updated 2024-2025)

Per HB 939 and F.S. 626.8796:
- Written contract mandatory, minimum 12-point type
- Title: "Public Adjuster Contract"
- Must include: PA's full name, business address, license number, phone, email
- Must include: licensed public adjusting firm name and license number
- Must include: insured's details, description of loss, insurer name, policy number (if available)
- Must include: compensation details and date of contract execution
- **Cancellation right**: 10 business days after execution (standard)
- **Emergency cancellation**: 30 days after loss or 10 days post-execution, whichever is longer, without penalty

### 1.4 Solicitation Rules

- Permitted: Monday-Saturday, 8:00 AM - 8:00 PM only
- Must comply with all DFS advertising and solicitation regulations

### 1.5 Dual Licensing Prohibition

- A PA cannot simultaneously hold company adjuster or independent adjuster licenses
- Cannot represent a claimant on a claim previously adjusted as a company/independent adjuster (and vice versa)

## 2. FL ADMINISTRATIVE CODE -- ETHICAL REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1 Rule 69B-220.201 (Effective April 21, 2025)

**Core Principle**: "An adjuster shall put the duty for fair and honest treatment of the claimant above the adjuster's own interests in every instance."

**General Ethics (All Adjusters)**:
1. Avoid undisclosed financial conflicts when referring claimants for repairs/services
2. Treat all claimants equally without favoritism
3. Adjust claims strictly per insurance contract terms
4. Conduct complete, truthful investigations
5. Operate with honesty and integrity
6. Act promptly and diligently on all claims
7. Respect attorney representation of third-party claimants
8. Never discourage claimants from seeking legal counsel
9. Avoid negotiations during trauma or distress
10. Maintain current competency for claim types handled
11. Promptly report violations by other licensees to DFS

**PA-Specific Ethics**:
1. Cannot prevent/dissuade claimant from speaking privately with insurer, company adjuster, independent adjuster, attorney, or any other person
2. Cannot accept power of attorney giving PA authority to choose repair contractors
3. Must provide written contracts with all terms per Rule 69B-220.051 and F.S. 626.854/626.8796
4. Cannot exceed statutory compensation limits
5. Must respond within 30 days to claims status inquiries
6. Violation = grounds for administrative action AND constitutes unfair claims settlement practice

### 2.2 Insurer Obligations (Relevant to PA Practice)

- Insurers must provide 48 hours notice before scheduling on-site inspections
- Insurers must acknowledge claims within 14 days
- Insurers must pay or deny claims within 60 days (post-SB 2A, previously 90)
- Physical inspections required within 30 days
- Adjuster reports must be provided within 7 days
- Adjusters must identify appointment type in all communications (per HB 989)

## 3. MAJOR LEGISLATION AFFECTING PAs (2022-2025)

### 3.1 SB 2A (December 2022) -- Landmark Reform

| Change | Impact on PAs |
|--------|--------------|
| AOB banned (post Jan 1, 2023) | Eliminates contractor-driven claims; PAs become primary policyholder advocates |
| One-way attorney fees repealed | Each party covers own legal costs; reduces litigation-driven claims |
| Claim filing window: 2 years -> 1 year | PAs must engage faster; no delayed reopenings |
| Supplemental claim deadline: 18 months | PAs must document and supplement promptly |
| Insurer response: 90 days -> 60 days | Faster resolution cycle benefits diligent PAs |
| Bad faith threshold raised | Must demonstrate genuine misconduct, not routine disputes |

### 3.2 HB 837 (2023) -- Tort Reform

- Limits bad faith claims if insurer pays policy limits or claim amount within 90 days with sufficient evidence
- Eliminates remaining one-way attorney fee provisions

### 3.3 HB 1185 (2023)

- PA fees capped at 1% when claims equal or exceed policy limits
- Solar panel sellers must disclose roof replacement impacts and insurance implications

### 3.4 SB 7052 (2023) -- OIR Enhancement

- Mandatory 90-day post-hurricane market conduct examinations
- Penalties increased 250-500%
- Insurer response time reduced to 14 days (from 20)

### 3.5 HB 939 (2024) -- Contract & Claims Procedures

- PA contracts must include firm license number
- Notice of Change in Policy Terms must be in bold 14-point type
- Loss assessment claims have specific filing deadlines

### 3.6 HB 989 (2024)

- Adjusters must identify appointment type in all communications
- Surplus lines companies must respond to complaints within 14 days

### 3.7 2025 Session Bills

- **SB 948**: Enhanced flood disclosures for landlords/sellers
- **HB 393**: Restricts My Safe Florida Condominium Pilot Program
- **HB 715**: Expands roofing contractor scope; narrows cancellation window to 10 days during emergencies
- **HB 913**: Strengthens condominium oversight (replacement value insurance, manager licensing, reserves)

### 3.8 Litigation Trends (Post-Reform)

- Personal insurance litigation fell ~25% in H1 2025 vs. prior year
- Total decline ~36% since 2021
- Average requested rate hikes: 21% (2023) -> 0.2% (2025)
- Fewer late-emerging claims on older catastrophe events
- Plaintiff bar adapting: promoting lawsuits as "first-line response" with reframed "homeowner rights" messaging

## 6. UNITED POLICYHOLDERS -- CONSUMER ADVOCACY INTELLIGENCE

### 6.1 First Steps After Hurricane/Flood

1. **Document first**: Take photos BEFORE cleanup, disposal, or repairs
2. **Daily journal**: Record all conversations (names, phone numbers, emails)
3. **Prevent further damage**: Only temporary repairs (cover broken windows, holes)
4. **Don't make permanent repairs** until instructed by insurance company
5. **Save all receipts** for emergency repairs
6. **Get complete policy copy**: Review all coverages and deductibles
7. **Register with FEMA** regardless of insurance status (documentation purposes)
8. **File flood claim within 60 days** if NFIP policy applies

### 6.2 Wind vs. Flood Coverage (Critical Distinction)

| Damage Type | Typically Covered By |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Wind-driven rain | Standard homeowner's |
| Tree damage from wind | Standard homeowner's |
| Roof/siding water intrusion | Standard homeowner's (wind as triggering cause) |
| Storm surge | Flood policy (may argue wind as precursor) |
| Standing water | Flood policy |
| Spoiled food (power outage) | Standard homeowner's |
| Power surge damage | Standard homeowner's |
| Vehicle damage | Comprehensive auto |

### 6.3 Claim Handling Strategies

**Adjuster Interactions**:
- Walk around with the insurer's adjuster during inspection
- Bring your contractor/builder to discuss estimates
- Proceed cautiously -- "Insurance companies are profit-making businesses and their employees are not social workers"
- Adjusters prioritize closing claims quickly with minimal payouts

**Documentation Strategy**:
- Three-ring binder organization system
- Don't rush contents inventories -- "It's normal not to remember much after a traumatic loss"
- Use UP's sample inventory forms
- Claim associated costs (taxes, shipping, transportation)

**Dispute Resolution Options** (in escalation order):
1. Written escalation to company executives
2. Mediation (informal, pre-suit)
3. Appraisal process (mini-trial without jury)
4. Insurance Commissioner complaint
5. Hiring forensic engineers
6. Pooling resources with neighbors
7. Media attention and elected officials
8. Litigation (last resort)

### 6.4 Key Negotiation Points for PAs

- **Depreciation**: Challenge subjective decisions; structural materials (studs, framing, rebar) shouldn't depreciate
- **Matching**: Insurers cannot avoid matching siding, roofing, or carpeting -- restoration requires "uniform and consistent" appearance
- **Overhead & Profit**: Standard contractor costs (typically 10% + 10%) should not be withheld upfront
- **Real-world pricing**: "Your insurance company owes you for what it will cost in real life, not on paper"
- **ACV vs. RCV**: Request receipts to recover difference between Actual Cash Value and replacement costs
- **Supplemental claims**: Submit for price increases; keep all receipts

### 6.5 Timing Warning

"It's still very early in the process -- way too early to under-settle." Never accept early lowball offers.

## 7. FL MEDIATION & APPRAISAL PROCESSES

### 7.1 Mediation (F.S. 627.7015)

| Step | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Trigger | Insurer MUST offer mediation before demanding appraisal |
| Notification | Insurer must notify homeowners of right to participate |
| 21-day review | Insurance company reviews file, may attempt settlement |
| Mediator assignment | Conference within 21 calendar days of assignment |
| Attendance | All policyholders listed on claim must attend |
| Rescission right | 3 business days to rescind (if check not cashed) |
| Cost | Free to policyholder (state-funded program) |

### 7.2 Appraisal Process

| Step | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Appraisers | Each side selects one appraiser |
| Umpire | Both appraisers select a mutual neutral umpire |
| Decision | If appraisers disagree, umpire reviews and decides |
| Cost | Umpire and appraisal expenses split equally |
| Binding | Generally binding on both parties |

## 8. CARRIER MISCONDUCT PATTERNS (Intelligence for Eve)

### 8.1 Documented Violations (FL OIR Enforcement)

**Monarch National Insurance Company** (Consent Order, August 2025):
- Used unlicensed adjusters for Hurricane Ian and Idalia claims
- Failed to acknowledge claims within 14-day statutory period
- Omitted required disclosure language from 1,000+ preliminary estimates
- Missed 90-day deadlines for claim payment/denial
- Fine: $325,000 + administrative costs
- Paradox: Approved to assume 25,000 Citizens policies just 6 days before fine

**Eight insurers total** fined over $2M for Hurricane Ian/Idalia misconduct.

### 8.2 Common Carrier Denial/Underpayment Patterns

1. **ACV underpayment**: Paying minimal ACV estimates, forcing policyholders to prove insufficiency (Bailetti v. Universal Property, Oct 2025 -- FL 1st DCA)
2. **Excessive depreciation**: Subjective depreciation on materials that shouldn't depreciate
3. **Matching denial**: Refusing to pay for matching materials (siding, roofing, carpet)
4. **O&P withholding**: Holding back standard 10%+10% overhead and profit
5. **Scope narrowing**: Minimizing scope of loss to reduce payouts
6. **Timeline violations**: Missing statutory deadlines for acknowledgment, inspection, and payment
7. **Unlicensed adjuster deployment**: Using unqualified personnel for field inspections
8. **Paper pricing**: Using internal pricing guides rather than real-world contractor costs
9. **Anti-concurrent causation clauses**: Denying wind damage claims by attributing to excluded flood
10. **Delayed inspections**: Using 30-day inspection window strategically

### 8.3 Critical Legal Precedent: Bailetti v. Universal Property (Oct 2025)

- FL 1st DCA held insurers satisfy obligation by paying "at least one reasonable ACV estimate"
- Burden shifts to policyholder to prove payment was insufficient
- Later estimates produced years after loss cannot prove earlier ACV payment was inadequate when made
- **PA Implication**: Document and challenge ACV contemporaneously -- do NOT delay evidence gathering

## 9. BEST PRACTICES FOR PAs (Synthesized Across All Sources)

### 9.1 Claims Handling Excellence

1. **Engage within days** -- 1-year filing window means speed matters
2. **Document exhaustively** from day one -- photos, videos, written notes
3. **Contemporaneous evidence** -- ACV challenges must happen early (Bailetti ruling)
4. **Walk every inspection** with the carrier's adjuster
5. **Bring contractor/engineer** to inspections for technical backup
6. **Detailed written letters** explaining disagreements with supporting evidence before any litigation
7. **Supplemental claims** filed promptly -- 18-month supplemental deadline
8. **Track all statutory deadlines** -- 14-day acknowledgment, 30-day inspection, 60-day pay/deny
9. **Know the policy language** -- read every word, not just coverage summary
10. **Distinguish wind vs. flood causation** accurately for each damage element

### 9.2 Client Communication

1. Never prevent client from speaking with insurer or anyone else
2. Explain fee structure transparently (10%/20% limits, 1% at policy limits)
3. Set realistic timeline expectations
4. Provide regular status updates (respond within 30 days per rule)
5. Educate client on their rights and the process

### 9.3 Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Valid 3-20 license current
- [ ] $50K surety bond active
- [ ] Written contract with all required elements (12pt, titled correctly)
- [ ] Fee within statutory limits (10%/20%/1%)
- [ ] Solicitation within permitted hours (Mon-Sat, 8AM-8PM)
- [ ] No dual licensing conflicts
- [ ] CE current (24hrs/2yrs or reduced schedule)
- [ ] Law & Ethics CE (4hrs, 5-320) completed
- [ ] No undisclosed financial conflicts in referrals
- [ ] 30-day response to status inquiries

### 9.4 Post-Reform Strategy Adaptations

1. **AOB is dead** -- PAs are now the primary advocacy channel for policyholders
2. **No one-way fees** -- litigation economics changed; strong documentation pre-suit is critical
3. **Shorter timelines** -- faster engagement, faster evidence gathering, faster supplements
4. **Mediation first** -- use state mediation program before appraisal or litigation
5. **Carrier compliance monitoring** -- track insurer deadline violations as leverage
6. **Contemporaneous ACV challenges** -- per Bailetti, delayed evidence is worthless
