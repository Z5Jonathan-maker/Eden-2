## 1. Deadlines & Timeframes

### Policyholder Claim Filing Deadlines (F.S. 627.70132)

| Claim Type | Deadline | Measured From |
|------------|----------|---------------|
| Initial / Reopened claim | **1 year** | Date of loss |
| Supplemental claim | **18 months** | Date of loss |
| Loss assessment (condo) | Later of: 1 year from loss OR 90 days from assessment vote (max 3 years) | Date of loss |
| Military deployment | Tolled during combat zone deployment | N/A |

**Date of loss for weather events:** Date hurricane made landfall or weather event verified by NOAA.

**Key definitions:**
- **Reopened claim:** Previously closed claim reopened for additional costs on previously disclosed loss/damage
- **Supplemental claim:** Additional loss from same peril on an open claim for which timely notice was previously given

### Insurer Response Deadlines (F.S. 627.70131)

| Action | Deadline | Statute |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Acknowledge receipt of claim communication | **7 calendar days** | 627.70131 |
| Begin investigation after proof-of-loss | **7 days** | 627.70131 |
| Physical inspection of property | **30 days** after proof-of-loss | 627.70131 |
| Send adjuster's estimate to policyholder | **7 days** after estimate generated | 627.70131 |
| Pay or deny claim (initial, reopened, or supplemental) | **60 days** after notice received | 627.70131 |
| Emergency extension of pay/deny deadline | **+30 additional days** | 627.70131 |
| Send Homeowner Claims Bill of Rights | **14 days** after initial claim communication | 627.7142 |
| Interest accrues on late payment | From date insurer received notice of claim | 627.70131 / 55.03 |

### PA Contract Deadlines

| Action | Deadline | Statute |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Submit copy of PA contract to insurer | **7 days** after execution | 626.8796 |
| PA must provide written estimate to insurer | **60 days** after contract execution | 626.8796 |
| Insured rescission (normal) | **10 days** after contract execution | 626.854 |
| Insured rescission (emergency declaration) | **30 days** from loss OR **10 days** from execution (whichever longer) | 626.854 / 626.8796 |
| Insured rescission if PA fails 60-day estimate | **Any time** (contract voidable) | 626.8796 |

### Civil Remedy / Bad Faith Deadlines

| Action | Deadline | Statute |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Civil Remedy Notice (CRN) before bad faith suit | **60 days** before filing | 624.155 |
| Insurer safe harbor: tender policy limits | **90 days** of claim notice (liability) | 624.155(4)(a) |
| Rescind mediation settlement | **3 business days** (if check not cashed) | 627.7015 |

### Statute of Limitations (F.S. 95.11)

| Action | Limitation | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Breach of property insurance contract | **5 years** from date of loss | 95.11(2)(e) |
| Bad faith action | Subject to CRN 60-day tolling | 624.155 |

## 2. Public Adjuster Rights & Requirements

### Definition (F.S. 626.854)

A public adjuster is any person who, for compensation, prepares, completes, or files insurance claims; negotiates settlements; or solicits adjusting business on behalf of insureds or claimants.

**Exemptions from PA licensing:**
- Licensed attorneys
- Health care providers assisting with health insurance
- Licensed health insurance agents

### Licensing Requirements (F.S. 626.865)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Age | 18+ years, US citizen or legal alien with work authorization |
| Character | Trustworthy, reliable character and business reputation |
| Experience | 6 months continuous prior licensure as: nonresident PA, PA apprentice, independent adjuster, or company employee adjuster |
| Bond | **$50,000 surety bond** (remains effective 1 year post-license termination; 30 days written notice before cancellation) |
| Examination | Must pass department examination (no exemptions for reinstatement) |

### Apprentice PA Requirements (F.S. 626.8651)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Prerequisites | All-lines adjuster license + $50,000 surety bond |
| Firm limit | Maximum **4 apprentices** per firm simultaneously |
| Supervision | Each supervising adjuster oversees **only 1 apprentice** |
| Authority | Same as licensed PA EXCEPT cannot execute service contracts |
| Accountability | Supervising adjuster bears accountability for apprentice conduct |

### Prohibitions (F.S. 626.854)

| Prohibition | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Legal advice | Cannot advise on bodily injury, death, or noneconomic damages |
| Solicitation hours | Monday-Saturday, 8 AM - 8 PM only |
| Fee caps | 10-20% of claim payments (lower during emergencies) |
| Referral compensation | Cannot compensate non-adjusters for referrals |
| Advances/gifts | Cannot provide loans or merchandise exceeding **$25** |
| Property access | Insured can deny access without **48-hour notice** |
| Contractor conflict | Cannot participate in reconstruction/repair of claimed property (F.S. 626.8795) |

### Conflict of Interest (F.S. 626.8795)

A public adjuster **may not** participate, directly or indirectly, in:
- Reconstruction, repair, or restoration of property subject to a claim they adjust
- Soliciting or accepting compensation connected to repair/salvage entities
- Maintaining financial interests in businesses connected to claims they adjust

## 3. Carrier Duties & Obligations

### Claims Handling Standards (F.S. 626.9541(1)(i))

Insurers must:
- **Acknowledge and act promptly** on all claim communications
- **Affirm or deny coverage** (full or partial) within specified timeframes
- **Adopt standards for proper investigation** of claims
- **Not deny claims without reasonable investigation** based on available information
- **Provide written explanation** of basis in policy for any denial or settlement offer
- **Promptly notify** insured of additional information needed
- **Pay undisputed amounts** within 60 days of residential property claim notice

**Unfair Claim Settlement Practices** include:
- Failing to acknowledge communications promptly
- Failing to conduct reasonable investigations
- Denying claims without stating basis in policy
- Compelling litigation by offering substantially less than amounts due
- Making claim payments without coverage statement
- Failing to promptly settle claims when liability is clear

### Insurer Investigation Duties (F.S. 627.70131)

The 7-7-30-60 framework:
1. **7 days:** Acknowledge claim receipt
2. **7 days:** Begin investigation after proof-of-loss
3. **30 days:** Conduct physical inspection
4. **60 days:** Pay or deny the claim

Supplemental claims receive the **same timeline protections** as original claims.

**Interest provision:** Any payment made after the 60-day deadline bears interest at the statutory rate set forth in F.S. 55.03, accruing from when the insurer received notice of the claim.

### Deductible & Coinsurance Disclosure (F.S. 627.701)

- Hurricane deductible options: $500, 2%, 5%, or 10% of dwelling limits
- Roof deductibles: Capped at lesser of 2% of Coverage A or 50% of replacement cost
- **18-point boldfaced warnings** about deductibles required in policy documents
- Dollar amounts must be displayed prominently
- Written policyholder acknowledgment required

### Roof-Related Duties

- Cannot deny coverage solely based on roof age if roof is **under 15 years old** (SB 2D, 2022)
- Roofs 15+ years may require inspection showing 5+ years remaining useful life
- HB 815 (eff. July 1, 2026): Prohibits denial/nonrenewal based solely on roof age without considering actual condition; requires differentiation between steep-slope and low-slope roofs

### Data Reporting (HB 1611, eff. July 1, 2024)

- **Monthly** policy and claims data submission to OIR (previously quarterly)
- Quarterly and annual transparency reporting on deductions, discounts, and policy impacts (SB 7052)

### Post-Hurricane Restrictions (SB 7052 / HB 1611)

- Cannot cancel/non-renew residential properties damaged in hurricanes for **90 days after repair completion**
- OIR may conduct market conduct examinations **90 days post-hurricane**
- Emergency-related violations carry **500% maximum penalty increase**

## 4. Policyholder Rights

### Homeowner Claims Bill of Rights (F.S. 627.7142)

Must be provided within **14 days** of initial claim communication. Rights include:

1. **Claim acknowledgment** within 7 days
2. **Coverage determination** within 30 days of complete proof-of-loss (written confirmation of full/partial coverage, denial, or investigation status)
3. **Estimate access** -- copy of adjuster's estimate within 7 days of generation
4. **Settlement timeline** -- within 60 days: full payment, undisputed portion, or written denial
5. **Interest payment** -- accrues if payment/denial exceeds 60 days
6. **Free mediation** through Florida DFS
7. **Sinkhole neutral evaluation** for sinkhole damage claims
8. **Consumer assistance** -- DFS Division of Consumer Services helpline

**Limitation:** The Bill of Rights does not create civil causes of action or enlarge statutory requirements.

### Contract Cancellation Rights

| Situation | Cancellation Period |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Normal PA contract | 10 days after execution |
| Emergency-declared PA contract | 30 days from loss OR 10 days from execution (whichever longer) |
| PA fails to submit estimate within 60 days | Any time (contract voidable) |
| Roofing contract after state of emergency | 10 days after signing (HB 939/715) |
| Vulnerable adult / incapacitated person | **Any time** (eff. July 1, 2026) |

### Flood Disclosure Rights

- **HB 1049 (eff. Oct 1, 2024):** Sellers must disclose flood claims and federal disaster assistance history
- **SB 948 (eff. Oct 1, 2025):** Landlords must disclose flood damage history to tenants; tenants may terminate leases within 30 days of substantial flood damage

### Arbitration Rights (SB 2A)

- Mandatory arbitration endorsements require **policyholder consent**
- Premium discount required for policies with arbitration clauses
- Policies without arbitration must also be offered

## 5. Dispute Resolution (Mediation & Appraisal)

### Mediation (F.S. 627.7015)

**Scope:** Personal lines and commercial residential policies. Excludes commercial coverages, motor vehicle, and liability disputes.

**Who may request:** Policyholders, third-party assignees, and insurers. Insurers not obligated to participate in third-party assignee requests.

**Eligibility:** Claims eligible after insurer complies with notice requirements. If insurer hasn't complied within **90 days after notice of loss**, insurer cannot require mediation.

**Cost:** **Insurer bears all mediation costs** ($350 per session). If policyholder no-shows, policyholder pays rescheduling. If insurer no-shows without good cause, insurer pays policyholder's actual expenses.

**Nature:** Nonbinding. Written settlements become binding unless rescinded within **3 business days** (if payment not cashed/deposited).

**Exclusions:** Fraud suspected, no coverage, material misrepresentation, amounts under $500, or non-compliant losses.

**DFS Mediation Programs:**

| Program | Minimum Amount | Eligible Disputes |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Residential | $500+ after deductible | Damage causation or amount disagreements |
| Commercial Residential | $500+ after deductible | Condo/HOA/Co-op policy disputes |
| Automobile | Any amount (property) / up to $10K (BI) | Vehicle ownership/operation claims |

### Appraisal

**Key Supreme Court Ruling -- American Coastal Insurance Co. v. San Marco Villas (Feb 2024):**
- Trial courts have **discretion** in determining order of coverage vs. amount-of-loss resolution
- Appraisal can be **compelled before coverage disputes are fully resolved**
- Insurers retain right to deny claims post-appraisal
- Overturned prior 4th DCA decisions requiring coverage resolution first

**Binding nature:** Appraisal awards are binding. Under Eleventh Circuit precedent, insurers cannot challenge discrete components of an appraisal award -- must dispute policy-wide coverage or invoke standard conditions.

**OIR enforcement:** OIR has discretionary authority to suspend/revoke insurer certificates or issue fines if insurer engages in general practice of compelling appraisal without just cause.

### Proposed DOAH Tribunal (2025-2026)

**SB 1508 / HB 1087:** Would replace current appraisal/mediation with state Division of Administrative Hearings process.

**HB 459 (2026):** Establishes formal administrative process for disputed claims. Insurer must respond within deadline or pay requested amount. ALJ issues final coverage determination.

## 6. Bad Faith & Civil Remedy

### Civil Remedy Notice (F.S. 624.155)

**Who can file:** Any person may bring civil action against an authorized insurer for damages.

**Violations covered:**
- Violations of F.S. 626.9541, 626.9551, 626.9705, 626.9706, 626.9707, or 627.7283
- Failure to attempt good faith claim settlement
- Paying claims without coverage statements
- Failing to promptly settle claims to influence other settlements

**60-Day Cure Period (Condition Precedent):**
- Must electronically file Form DFS-10-363 via https://apps.fldfs.com/civilremedy
- No filing fee
- Must specify: statutory provision violated, facts, circumstances, individuals involved, relevant policy language
- **CRN must state cause of action with specificity** -- citing an entire policy section is insufficient

**Safe Harbor (Liability Claims -- F.S. 624.155(4)(a)):**
Bad faith action shall not lie if insurer tenders lesser of policy limits or demanded amount within **90 days** of receiving notice with sufficient supporting evidence. Post-2023, this applies when cause of action accrues after March 24, 2023.

**Damages:** Prevailing plaintiffs recover damages, court costs, and reasonable attorney fees. Punitive damages require proof of willful, wanton, malicious, or recklessly disregardful conduct occurring with frequency indicating general business practice.

### Post-2023 Bad Faith Standards (HB 837 / SB 2A)

**Higher Bar Established:**
- **Court-ordered breach finding required:** Must obtain final judgment or decree finding insurer breached the insurance contract BEFORE filing bad faith action
- **More than negligence:** Must prove insurer's conduct amounts to intentional conduct (not mere negligence)
- **Mutual good faith standard (F.S. 624.155(5)(b)):** Insured, policyholder, and their agents (including PA) also held to good faith standard -- insurers can point to PA/insured conduct as reason settlement wasn't achieved
- **Jury consideration:** Jury may consider claimant's, insured's, or PA's actions as a reason settlement was not achieved

### Citizens Property Insurance & Bad Faith

**Citizens Property Insurance Corp. v. Perdido Sun (2015):** Florida Supreme Court ruled Citizens **cannot** be sued for bad faith by policyholders.

**Citizens v. Manor House, LLC (2021):** Citizens cannot be held liable for consequential damages from claim handling failures.

**Practical impact:** Bad faith remedies are significantly limited for Citizens policyholders.

## 7. SB 2A (2022) Tort Reform Impact

Signed December 16, 2022 by Governor DeSantis. The most sweeping insurance reform in Florida history.

### Assignment of Benefits (AOB) Ban

- **Prohibited** assignment of any post-loss insurance benefit under residential or commercial property policies issued **on or after January 1, 2023**
- Any attempt to assign post-loss benefits is **void, invalid, and unenforceable**
- AOB lawsuits dropped from 28,200 (2016 peak) to near-zero post-2023

### One-Way Attorney Fees Eliminated

- **Repealed** the automatic fee recovery for prevailing policyholders
- Restored mutual "offer of judgment" procedures
- Each party now bears own legal costs unless offer-of-judgment thresholds met
- **NOT retroactive:** Blumberg v. Security First (5th DCA, Aug 2025) confirmed fees not retroactive to pre-2022 policies

### Claims Timeline Changes

| Before SB 2A | After SB 2A |
|---------------|-------------|
| 14-day acknowledgment | **7-day** acknowledgment |
| 14-day investigation start | **7-day** investigation start |
| 2-year claim filing | **1-year** claim filing |
| 3-year supplemental claim | **18-month** supplemental claim |
| No inspection deadline | **30-day** physical inspection |

### Bad Faith Litigation Overhaul

- Breach of contract finding required before bad faith suit
- Jury considers all parties' conduct (including PA)
- 90-day safe harbor for insurers who tender promptly
- Intentional conduct standard (not mere negligence)

### Market Impact

| Metric | Before (2023) | After (2025) |
|--------|---------------|--------------|
| Average rate hike requests | 21% | 0.2% |
| New insurance carriers | -- | 17 entered Florida |
| Citizens policies | 1.5M+ | Under 400,000 |
| Litigation drop | Baseline | -36% since 2021 |
| New claims (Sept 2024-2025) | -- | Down 80% |

## 8. Recent Case Law (2024-2026)

### American Coastal Insurance Co. v. San Marco Villas Condo Assn (FL Supreme Court, Feb 2024)

**Holding:** Trial courts have discretion to order appraisal before coverage disputes are resolved. Appraisal can be compelled even when coverage issues remain open.

**Impact:** Overturned 4th DCA's prior requirement that coverage must be determined first. Insurers retain post-appraisal coverage defense rights.

### Portofino Condo v. Insurers (N.D. Fla., 2025)

**Holding:** Dismissed first-party bad faith claims under F.S. 624.1551 because insurers paid appraisal awards before any adverse court judgment.

**Key rule:** Retroactive application determined by when cause of action accrues, not policy inception date. Claims accruing after SB 2A effective date are subject to new requirements.

### Direct General v. Creamer (M.D. Fla., 2025)

**Holding:** The 90-day safe harbor investigation period applies when bad faith causes of action accrue post-March 24, 2023, even if underlying policies predate reforms.

### Blumberg v. Security First Insurance Co. (5th DCA, Aug 28, 2025)

**Holding:** SB 2A's limitations on attorney fees **cannot be applied retroactively** to policies issued before the law took effect.

**Reasoning:** (1) SB 2A contains no retroactivity language; (2) Attorney fees are a substantive right. Policyholders with pre-2022 policies retain fee recovery rights.

### Universal Property & Casualty v. Naze (4th DCA, June 4, 2025)

**Holding:** Reversed jury verdict for insured -- trial court improperly admitted irrelevant claims handling and bad faith evidence.

### Hurricane Sally Bad Faith Litigation (2024-2025)

Condo associations demanded appraisal after Hurricane Sally (2020). Appraisal awards issued Feb-July 2023. Insurers paid policy limits but not interest/fees/costs. Associations filed bad faith suit September 2024. **Court dismissed** -- insurers paid before adverse judgment, so new F.S. 624.1551 standard applies.

### Litigation Trends

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Personal insurance litigation (H1 2025 vs H1 2024) | Down **25%** |
| Litigation 2023-2024 | Down **23%** |
| Cumulative decline since 2021 | Down **36%** |

## 9. 2025-2026 Legislative Changes

### Enacted Laws

**HB 815 -- Roof Protections (eff. July 1, 2026)**
- Prohibits insurers from denying/refusing renewal based solely on roof age without considering actual condition
- Requires differentiation between steep-slope and low-slope roofs

**HB 459 -- Disputed Claims Resolution (2026 implementation)**
- Formal administrative process through Division of Administrative Hearings
- Either party can petition; insurer must respond or pay
- ALJ issues binding coverage determination

**SB 948 -- Tenant Flood Disclosure (eff. Oct 1, 2025)**
- Landlords must disclose flood damage history
- Tenants may terminate within 30 days of substantial flood damage

**HB 715 -- Roofing Services (eff. May 19, 2025)**
- Licensed contractors may evaluate/enhance roof-to-wall connections during replacements
- Enhancements inspected per Uniform Mitigation Verification Inspection Form

**HB 913 -- Condo Insurance (eff. July 1, 2025)**
- Replacement value coverage required for condo association policies
- Appraisals must be updated minimum every 3 years

**Vulnerable Adult PA Contract Rescission (eff. July 1, 2026)**
- Vulnerable adults, incapacitated adults, and their legal reps may rescind PA contracts **at any time**
- PA firms must include firm license number on all contracts

### Pending / In Progress

**HB 527 -- AI Insurance Denial Ban (passed House 108-0; SB 202 companion failed committee)**
- Prohibits AI/algorithms/ML as **sole basis** for claim denial or reduction
- Requires human professional to make denial decisions
- Insurers must document AI's role in claims handling manuals
- Denial notices must identify human decision-maker and indicate if AI contributed
- **Effective date if enacted:** July 1, 2026

**HB 1551 / SB 426 -- Attorney Fee Restoration**
- Would restore fee-shifting for prevailing parties in property insurance disputes
- If homeowner judgment exceeds insurer's last offer, insurer pays homeowner's fees
- If insurer's offer exceeds judgment, homeowner could owe insurer's fees
- Status: Advanced through House committees as of June 2025

**SB 554 -- Comprehensive Claims Reform (Sen. Don Gaetz)**
- Sliding-scale attorney fees (80/20 formula)
- Mandatory mediation before lawsuits on disputed denials
- Initial written estimates within 7 days of claim notice
- Monthly itemized claim updates
- 10-day insurer response deadline to settlement demands
- Expanded OIR rate-disapproval authority
- Public disclosure of executive compensation

**SB 1534 -- Litigation Finance Transparency**
- Mandatory disclosures by third-party litigation funders
- Court-filing requirements

**SB 794 / HB 1555 -- Human Claims Review**
- Require qualified human professionals to sign all claim denials

**SB 1740 -- Insurer Financial Requirements**
- $35M minimum capital reserves
- Ban executives from previously failed insurers

## 10. Administrative Rules (69B / 69J)

### Rule 69B-220.051 -- Conduct of Public Adjusters

**Solicitation:**
- Only licensed PAs or apprentices under supervision may solicit business
- Unlicensed persons cannot solicit even under supervision
- Unlicensed staff may answer phones for purely administrative matters only

**Advertising:**
- Must include full name and license number of PA
- Multi-adjuster firms: designated primary adjuster's info required
- PA must personally review all ads for compliance
- Unlicensed spokespersons may appear if disclosed as such

**Contract requirements (per rule):**
- Insured's phone number
- Address of loss
- Brief loss description
- Insurance company name and policy number
- All methods of compensation, fees, and reimbursable costs
- Must comply with F.S. 626.854 and 626.8796

**Contract execution:**
- Only licensed PAs (not apprentices) may execute contracts
- Executed copy to insured at time of signing

**Settlement approval:**
- PA cannot accept settlement unless terms approved by insured/claimant

**Disclosure form:**
- Form DFS-H1-1982 must be provided before contract execution
- Form may only be modified to add PA/firm name, contact info, borders, or font (no content changes)

**Last amended:** April 29, 2024

### Rule 69B-220.201 -- Ethical Requirements for All Adjusters

**Core ethical standards:**
- Fair treatment of claimants above personal interests
- No undisclosed referral compensation
- Equal treatment of all claimants
- Unbiased investigations and truthful reporting
- Honest, prompt, diligent adjustments
- Cannot settle with represented third-party claimants without attorney consent
- Cannot discourage claimants from seeking legal counsel or PA services
- Cannot negotiate when claimants are in shock/emotional distress
- Must inform claimants of coverage options per contract
- Cannot adjust claims exceeding current expertise
- Must use electronic estimating programs for residential coverage
- Must respond to status inquiries within **30 days** with documentation

**PA-specific ethical constraints:**
- Cannot prevent claimant communication with insurer
- Cannot accept authority to choose repair contractors
- Cannot exceed statutory fee limits
- Cannot simultaneously hold company/independent adjuster licenses
- Cannot represent entities previously adjusted as insurer representative

**Violation:** Breaching any provision = grounds for administrative action AND constitutes unfair claims settlement practice.

### Rule 69J-123.002 -- Civil Remedy Notice

- Must be electronically filed on Form DFS-10-363
- Filed at: https://apps.fldfs.com/civilremedy
- No filing fee
- Filed **60 days** before bringing bad faith action
- Must state cause of action with specificity (citing entire policy section is insufficient)
- All written communications between parties can be electronically added to the record
- Insurer disposition reports added to same form

### Rule 69J-166 -- Mediation Programs

- Mediators must be Florida Supreme Court certified circuit court mediators
- Insurer pays all costs ($350/session)
- No-show policyholder pays rescheduling costs
- 3-business-day rescission window for settlements

## 11. Fee Limitations & Contract Requirements

### PA Fee Caps (F.S. 626.854)

| Scenario | Maximum Fee |
|----------|------------|
| Standard claim | **10%** of claim payment |
| Emergency-declared claim (varies) | **10%** or lower |
| Non-emergency, non-catastrophe | Up to **20%** (based on contract) |
| Claim payment equals/exceeds policy limits | **1%** of claim payment |
| Payments made before PA contract executed | **$0** (no fee allowed) |
| Supplemental/reopened claim | Based only on payments obtained through PA's work after contract |

### Contract Requirements (F.S. 626.8796)

**Format:**
- Written, minimum **12-point type**
- Title: "Public Adjuster Contract"
- Fraud warning: minimum **18-point bold type** before signature line
- Compensation percentage: **18-point bold type** before signatures

**Required information:**
- PA's full name, business address, phone, email, license number
- Firm's name and license number
- Insured's complete contact details
- Loss description
- All compensation terms

**Execution & delivery:**
- Unaltered copy to insured at signing
- Submitted to insurer within **7 days**
- PA cannot receive compensation until insured receives executed contract
- All non-signature pages must be **initialed by insured** (HB 1185)

**Disclosure document (required before signing):**
- Separate document explaining adjuster types
- States PA is not the insurer's employee
- Clarifies insured's rights to direct insurer communication
- Explains cost responsibility

**Non-compliance:** Non-compliant contracts are **invalid and unenforceable.**

### Additional Contract Rules (HB 1185, eff. July 1, 2023)

- Cannot contract for third-party services without insured's written consent
- License number required on all contracts
- Five-year record retention of signed contracts
- License must be posted at business location or carried when conducting business
- Phone number and email required on contract

## 12. Statute of Limitations

### Property Insurance Claims

| Action | Limitation Period | Starting Point | Statute |
|--------|------------------|----------------|---------|
| Notice of claim to insurer | **1 year** | Date of loss | 627.70132 |
| Notice of supplemental claim | **18 months** | Date of loss | 627.70132 |
| Breach of insurance contract (lawsuit) | **5 years** | Date of loss | 95.11(2)(e) |
| Bad faith civil action | Subject to 60-day CRN tolling | After breach finding | 624.155 |
| Condo loss assessment claim | Later of 1yr or 90 days from vote (max 3yr) | Date of loss | 627.70132(4) |

### Important Notes

- The **1-year / 18-month notice deadlines** (627.70132) are separate from the **5-year lawsuit limitation** (95.11)
- Filing a claim notice does not start the lawsuit clock -- the lawsuit SOL runs from date of loss
- Military deployment to combat zones **tolls** the filing deadlines
- Weather event date of loss = NOAA-verified date, not date damage discovered
- **SB 2A changed** the notice deadlines from 2 years / 3 years to 1 year / 18 months (effective Dec 2022)
- The 5-year breach of contract SOL was not changed by SB 2A

## Key Practical Takeaways for PA Operations

### What PAs Must Do
1. Execute written contracts in 12pt+ type with 18pt bold compensation and fraud warnings
2. Provide disclosure document BEFORE contract signing
3. Submit contract copy to insurer within 7 days
4. Provide written damage estimate to insurer within 60 days
5. Get insured approval before accepting any settlement
6. Maintain $50,000 surety bond at all times
7. Include license number on all contracts and advertisements
8. Retain signed contracts for 5 years
9. Respond to status inquiries within 30 days with documentation
10. Use electronic estimating programs for residential coverage

### What PAs Cannot Do
1. Participate in repair/reconstruction of claimed property
2. Solicit outside Monday-Saturday 8AM-8PM
3. Provide loans or gifts exceeding $25
4. Compensate non-adjusters for referrals
5. Choose repair contractors for insured
6. Prevent insured from communicating with their insurer
7. Exceed statutory fee caps
8. Collect fees for payments made before contract execution
9. Give legal advice on bodily injury/death/noneconomic damages
10. Hold simultaneous company/independent adjuster licenses

### What Insurers Must Do (PA Can Hold Them Accountable)
1. Acknowledge claims within 7 days
2. Begin investigation within 7 days
3. Inspect property within 30 days
4. Share adjuster estimates within 7 days of generation
5. Pay or deny within 60 days
6. Pay interest on late payments
7. Provide written denial explanations citing policy basis
8. Deliver Homeowner Claims Bill of Rights within 14 days
9. Bear all mediation costs ($350/session)
10. Comply with unfair claims settlement practices standards

### Red Flags That Trigger Regulatory / Legal Action
- Insurer fails to acknowledge claim within 7 days
- No inspection within 30 days
- Claim unpaid/undenied after 60 days (interest accrues)
- Insurer compels appraisal without just cause (OIR penalty risk)
- Claim denied without written explanation citing policy
- AI used as sole basis for denial (if HB 527 enacted)
- Claims closed without payment at 46.7% rate (systemic concern)
